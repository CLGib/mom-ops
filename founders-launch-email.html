Why Mom Ops Exists

I'm a working mom who wants to say yes to everything:

  • Help plan the classroom party
  • Volunteer at RMHC
  • Help plan the piano recital

But the reality was that I kept failing to show up for all of it.

The Turning Point

In January 2026, I brought in a real person to help with a project for the first time. I quickly realized what I was actually offloading:

  • Designing my daughter's piano recital invitations
  • Researching classroom crafts
  • Small things I could do myself, I just didn't have the time

Two things happened almost immediately:

  • My work got better because my mind finally had space again
  • I could start saying yes to more of the things that mattered to me as a mom

What I realized wasn't "I need a helper." It was "I need a system my family can run on." The first version of that system happened to be a human. But the real product was the structure underneath — repeatable playbooks for the work that always comes back. Meal planning. Camp research. Birthday parties. Teacher gifts. Travel.

So that's what we've been building: the operating system for modern family life. AI-powered playbooks and household agents, with real human support layered on when you want it. So every mom doesn't have to assemble it from scratch.

That's why Mom Ops exists.
